## Topics Covered

### Lagrangian Mechanics

The Euler-Lagrange equation:

$$ \frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}_i}\right) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial q_i} = 0 $$

where $L = T - V$ is the Lagrangian.

### Hamiltonian Mechanics

The Hamiltonian is defined via Legendre transform:

$$ H = \sum_i p_i \dot{q}_i - L $$

Hamilton's equations:

$$ \dot{q}_i = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_i}, \quad \dot{p}_i = -\frac{\partial H}{\partial q_i} $$

### Canonical Transformations

Transformations that preserve Hamilton's equations. Generated by a generating function $F$:

$$ p_i = \frac{\partial F}{\partial q_i}, \quad Q_i = \frac{\partial F}{\partial P_i} $$
